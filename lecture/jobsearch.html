Portfolio Project Expectations

  • Portfolio Projects are not only an excellent and intentional tool to reinforce your learning, they are the single most important piece oof your job search readiness.
  • You transition from 80+ hours a week of studying to building
  • Your projects show future employers you have the skills they need
  • Your job search can only start when your portfolio projects are completed to a certain degree and deemed ready!
  • All three projects will require a minimum level of completion by graduation
  • Completion will be determined by your instructors and your career coaches
  • You will showcase your projects to other students
  • Your projects will be hard, but when we get there, you will be ready

Why Projects

  • Solid GROUP projects demonstrate not only a mastery of the tech you've learned but your ability to work with a team -equitably- on a shared codebase
  • SOLO projects will be an integral part of the INTERVIEW PROCESS. You will be asked to walk through your code, explain your logic, and discuss major bugs/accomplishments along the way.

General Expectations Now - W16

  • Your INstructors are your guide. Take their advice and follow their direction
  • Start making Github commits on DAY ONE of Projects
    • Commit every 15-20 minutes or as changes are made
    • Push any time you finish a feature, step away for more than 10 min, or end for the day
    • recruiters and team leads will check to see if you have a good Github work ethic
  • Do NOT
    • Use 3rd party template/code snippets or follow tutorials
    • Copy code form another repo
    • Use technology outside the scope of the curriculum. Mastery is key
  • Do
    • Use the technology outlined in the curriculum + Lectures + videos
